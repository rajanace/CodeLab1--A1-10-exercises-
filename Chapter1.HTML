Sinigang na Bangus

Sinigang na Bangus is a traditional Filipino sour soup made with milkfish (bangus) and a variety of vegetables. The dish is known for its tangy flavor, which comes from tamarind or other souring agents. It's a comforting meal that is perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg bangus (milkfish), cleaned and cut into pieces
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 radish (labanos), sliced
  • 1 cup water spinach (kangkong)
  • 2-3 green chili peppers (siling pangsigang)
  • 1 cup tamarind paste or 1-2 cups fresh tamarind
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Method

  1. In a pot, combine the bangus, onion, tomatoes, and water. Bring to a boil.
  2. Add the tamarind paste or fresh tamarind and simmer for about 10 minutes.
  3. Add the radish and cook for another 5 minutes.
  4. Add the green chili peppers and water spinach. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot with rice.
